Scan quality

How to fix blurry scanned PDFs before converting

OCR quality depends heavily on the source scan. If the pages are blurry, skewed, or low contrast, conversion quality drops fast.

Home / Guides / Fix Blurry Scanned PDFs

People often blame the converter when the real problem is the scan. OCR cannot invent clean letterforms if the original page is fuzzy, shadowed, or distorted.

Look for the most common scan issues

  • Motion blur
  • Low contrast between text and paper
  • Skewed pages
  • Dark borders or page shadows
  • Photos taken at an angle instead of straight on

Fix clarity before OCR if you can

Improving the image first almost always beats trying to rescue bad OCR afterward. Even basic sharpening, contrast correction, and de-skewing can noticeably improve recognition.

Keep page edges clean

Heavy shadows and curved page edges confuse OCR engines. Cleaner margins help the system identify where text starts and stops, which also improves paragraph reconstruction later.

Use sample pages to judge readiness

Before converting a full document, test a few pages that represent the worst quality in the file. If those pages convert acceptably, the rest of the PDF is much more likely to work.

Then convert with the right workflow

Once the scan is readable, use the homepage converter for the hosted Python workflow or run the downloadable scripts locally if you want full control over the environment.
