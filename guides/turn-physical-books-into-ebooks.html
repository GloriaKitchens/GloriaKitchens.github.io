How to turn physical books into eBooks

Good conversion starts before OCR. The cleaner the scan, the better the final EPUB.

Home / Guides / Turn Physical Books Into eBooks

Turning a physical book into an ebook is really a chain of small tasks: scanning, cleanup, text recognition, formatting, and final packaging. If any step is rushed, the reading experience suffers.

Start with the cleanest capture you can

Even a good OCR engine struggles when pages are curved, dim, or cropped too tightly. Straight pages, even lighting, and sharp contrast matter more than most people expect.

Decide whether you need archive fidelity or reading comfort

If preserving the exact printed layout matters most, PDF may stay the final format. If your goal is comfortable reading on a phone, tablet, or e-reader, EPUB is usually worth the extra conversion work.

Clean up before OCR when possible

Small fixes like de-skewing, improving contrast, and removing heavy background noise can raise OCR accuracy much more than changing tools after the fact.

Choose the output based on how you plan to use it

  • Plain text for note extraction
  • Searchable PDF for archiving
  • EPUB for everyday reading

Use a conversion workflow that supports cleanup

A useful ebook workflow should do more than recognize text. It should help rebuild paragraphs, detect headings, and keep figure-heavy pages readable. That is why a dedicated PDF to EPUB pipeline is more useful than OCR alone for book-like documents.
