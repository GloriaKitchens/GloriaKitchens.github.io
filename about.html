About the site

A practical site built around one useful converter

This project centers on turning PDFs into more readable EPUB files while also publishing evergreen guides around OCR, scanning, and ebook workflows.

Home / About

What the site is for

Some people arrive with a scanned class handout. Others have an old government report, a personal archive, or a book they want to read more comfortably on a phone or e-reader. PDF is everywhere, but it is often awkward for long-form reading.

This site exists to make that transition easier. The browser frontend now sends jobs to a Python backend so the hosted tool can use the same higher-quality conversion pipeline as the downloadable scripts.

Who it helps

  • Students trying to make scanned course material searchable
  • Readers who prefer EPUB on phones or e-readers
  • Researchers cleaning up older PDFs for long reading sessions
  • Hobby archivists converting scans into more usable files

Why publish guides alongside the tool

Converters work best when they are part of a workflow. People often need to know how to improve a blurry scan, which OCR tool fits their situation, or whether EPUB is even the right format. The guide section answers those questions and links readers back to the converter when they are ready to act.

How the site can grow

The current focus stays narrow: one converter, one Python workflow, and a supporting guide library. Over time, related tools such as PDF to TXT or OCR text extraction can be added without changing the overall site structure.
